Overview

The Department of Computer Science & Engineering was established in 2005 and has grown into one of the largest departments at MLRIT. With NBA accreditation and autonomous status, the department offers a rigorous curriculum blending theoretical foundations with practical, industry-relevant skills.

What sets us apart is our emphasis on project-based learning, industry collaborations with companies like TCS, Infosys, and Wipro, and a dedicated placement cell that ensures students are career-ready from day one.

Vision & Mission

Vision: To be a centre of excellence in Computer Science & Engineering education and research, producing globally competent professionals who contribute to the advancement of technology and society.

Mission: To impart quality education through innovative teaching methodologies, foster research aptitude, develop industry-ready skills, and instil ethical values and social responsibility in students.

COs & POs

Program Outcomes (POs):

  • PO1 — Engineering Knowledge Apply knowledge of mathematics, science & engineering
  • PO2 — Problem Analysis Identify, formulate & analyse complex engineering problems
  • PO3 — Design & Development Design solutions for complex problems with societal consideration
  • PO4 — Modern Tool Usage Create, select & apply appropriate tools and techniques
  • PO5 — Ethics & Professionalism Apply ethical principles and professional responsibilities

Course Outcomes (COs):

  • CO1 — Data Structures & Algorithms Implement efficient solutions using DSA concepts
  • CO2 — Database Management Design and query relational databases effectively
  • CO3 — Operating Systems Understand process management, memory & file systems
  • CO4 — Machine Learning Build and evaluate ML models for real-world problems

Syllabus

  • Semester 1–2 Engineering Mathematics, Physics, C Programming, English
  • Semester 3–4 Data Structures, DBMS, Computer Organisation, OOP with Java
  • Semester 5–6 Operating Systems, Computer Networks, Software Engineering, AI/ML
  • Semester 7–8 Cloud Computing, Cyber Security, Major Project, Electives

Full syllabus available on the JNTUH autonomous curriculum portal. Updated annually to align with industry trends.
